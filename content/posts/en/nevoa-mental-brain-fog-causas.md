Reading the same paragraph three times and retaining nothing. Forgetting an obvious word mid-sentence. Feeling your thoughts wade through honey.

People living with this are usually told it is stress, age or "in their head." But brain fog is a real symptom, described in the scientific literature — and above all, it is an **investigable** one.

## What brain fog actually is

"Brain fog" is not a diagnosis: it is the name we give a set of cognitive complaints — slowed processing, working memory lapses, difficulty sustaining attention and early mental fatigue.

The central point: brain fog is a consequence. The correct clinical question is *of what*.

## The causes an investigation considers

### 1. Non-restorative sleep
Sleep apnea, sleep fragmentation and chronic insomnia degrade memory consolidation and attention. Full cognition is impossible with poor sleep — and many "brain fog" cases are, in fact, an undiagnosed sleep disorder.

### 2. Long COVID
Cognitive complaints are among the most frequent in post-COVID syndrome. Mechanisms under study include neuroinflammation, cerebral perfusion changes and autonomic dysfunction.

### 3. Dysautonomia and POTS
When circulatory regulation fails, the brain may receive less blood while standing. Brain fog that worsens through the day, upright or after exertion, raises this hypothesis.

### 4. Mast Cell Activation Syndrome
Inflammatory mediators like histamine also act on the central nervous system. MCAS patients frequently describe cognitive flares together with skin, gut and heart symptoms.

### 5. Metabolic and medication causes
Thyroid, iron and B12 deficiency, sedative effects of current medications. Common, treatable causes that must be actively excluded.

## How it is investigated

The logical sequence: detailed history (onset, daily pattern, triggers), objective sleep evaluation, orthostatic measurement, hypothesis-driven testing — and correlation with the body's other symptoms. Brain fog rarely comes alone; the whole set tells the story.

> Cognition is a whole-body function. Investigating brain fog means investigating the organism — not dismissing the complaint.
